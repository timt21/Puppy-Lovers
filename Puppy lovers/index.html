Puppy Parenting 101

Essential Puppy Care Guide

Welcoming a new puppy is an exciting journey. Here is a quick guide on the essentials to keep them healthy, safe, and happy.

Proper nutrition is vital during your puppy's first year of rapid growth. Keep these rules in mind:

  • Puppy-Specific Food: Always feed high-quality formula formulated specifically for puppies to ensure the correct calcium-phosphorus ratio.
  • Consistent Schedule: Puppies under 6 months should eat 3-4 times a day. Over 6 months, transition to twice daily.
  • Fresh Water: Always keep a clean bowl of fresh water accessible to prevent dehydration.

Building a strong bond and raising a well-mannered puppy starts with kind, consistent guidance:

  • Reward Good Behavior: Use small, soft treats and praise immediately when they perform a desired action. Ignore or redirect unwanted behaviors rather than punishing.
  • Potty Training: Establish a routine. Take your puppy out first thing in the morning, after meals, after naps, and before bed. Praise them when they go outside!
  • Keep Sessions Short: Puppies have short attention spans. Keep training sessions fun and limited to 5-10 minutes.

Protecting your pup's health ensures they grow up strong and disease-free:

  • Vaccination Schedule: Puppies require a series of vaccinations starting at 6-8 weeks, with booster shots every 3-4 weeks until they are about 16 weeks old.
  • De-worming & Flea Prevention: Consult your vet for an appropriate schedule of preventative treatments to protect against internal and external parasites.
  • Microchipping: Consider microchipping your puppy. It's a quick, safe procedure that significantly increases the chances of reuniting if they ever get lost.

Early socialization helps prevent future fear, anxiety, and aggression, shaping a confident adult dog:

  • The Golden Window: The most critical socialization period is between 3 and 16 weeks of age.
  • Gradual Exposure: Gently introduce them to a variety of people (different ages, sizes, wearing hats/coats), novel sounds (vacuum cleaner, doorbells, passing cars), and safe environments.
  • Vaccines First: Ensure they are sufficiently vaccinated before letting them socialize with unknown dogs or walk in high-traffic public parks.
